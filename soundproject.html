Moving Sound

I am interested in the relationships between sound and image, such as graphic scores that visually represent notes or the translation from an image to audio. I wanted to create an interactive piece that explores both senses of sight and hearing, and I ended up with these movable panels that make sounds as they rub against surfaces beneath them. The panels can also be rearranged to create layers with different visuals. The panels are wood and acrylic, and the materials that line the bottom are sandpaper, carpet, and patterns engraved on plywood. The pieces were designed in Illustrator and cut out using a laser cutter. Although I originally wanted to incorporate a score, I think it could be possible to use this piece as an instrument and create a score from it rather than the piece being a score itself. Another way to extend this project is to try making the panels from different materials or adding other materials on top of the panels, such as the addition of sandpaper or fabric. Furthermore, I observed that people interacted with the piece differently, being either more drawn to the visual or audible aspects of it, and it could be interesting to try to incorporate a more tangible, textural feature, too. I'd like to think of this as a prototype, but I am still pretty happy with how it turned out. At least I am very, very comfortable with the laser cutter now. I hope to continue experimenting with how we can experience sounds and images differently.
